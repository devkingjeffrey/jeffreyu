The Reconciliation Tax Checklist — Audit Your Data Before It Costs You

A five-section, working checklist for auditing donor, partner, and programme data before a reporting deadline forces the audit to happen the hard way. Pulled directly from the patterns covered in The Reconciliation Tax — go through it with your actual data open, not as a reading exercise.

What it covers

  • Data Source Inventory — every place your data actually lives, and who owns each one
  • Field Consistency — the mismatched names, date formats, and currencies that break reconciliation silently
  • Duplicate Risk — where the same donor or partner is likely entered more than once
  • Access & Security — whether sensitive records are actually restricted to the people who need them
  • Reporting Readiness — how long it currently takes to produce one number you'd hand to a funder

How to use it

Print it or open it alongside your systems and go section by section. Unchecked boxes aren't a grade — they're a map of exactly where the reconciliation tax is building up, and where to start.
